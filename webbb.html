About Rabies

Rabies is a viral disease that affects the central nervous system, leading to brain inflammation and, if untreated, death. It is most commonly spread through the bite of an infected animal.

Symptoms

  • Fever and headache
  • Excessive salivation
  • Muscle spasms
  • Confusion and aggression
  • Paralysis

Prevention

Prevention is key when it comes to rabies. Here are some measures you can take:

  • Vaccinate your pets regularly.
  • Avoid contact with wild or stray animals.
  • Seek immediate medical attention after any animal bite.

Treatment

Rabies is almost always fatal once symptoms appear. Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) is essential after an exposure:

  1. Wash the wound thoroughly with soap and water.
  2. Seek medical care immediately.
  3. Follow the PEP vaccination schedule recommended by your healthcare provider.
